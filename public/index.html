A Heart from Space is a tool for drawing together using GPS. By moving in space, each member of a group adjusts one point of a drawing, enabling the group to collectively determine the shape they want to hold.

About

The project stems from a conversation with Yo-Yo Ma around a previous project titled www.grindruberairbnb.exposed. This work gives each participant a series of commands to perform, which when performed together create a synchronized choreography. Mr Ma wondering if the system might be used to guide hundreds of thousands of people all across the midwest to create a giant heart that would be visible from space, as a symbol of solidarity and collectivity.

A Heart from Space is a scaled down version of this vision, with a focus on the spatial negotiations that the group performs in creating the heart. The combined hurdles of technical complexity and the dynamics of group behaviour make this task sometimes challenging, and the hearts that are created are not always perfect. But this incompleteness echos the difficulty that collaboration entails, and provides a grounding to the optimism of the project.

To perform the work, visit this website on a mobile phone. A simple heart can be made with 4 people, but best results are with groups of 8-10 people. When you've created a heart, share a screenshot of it with #aheartfromspace!
